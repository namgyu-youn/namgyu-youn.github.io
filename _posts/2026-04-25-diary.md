Recently, I suffered from Menhera (mental issue), caused by an uncontrollable situation. For a reminder, I would like to record "escape manual".


## 01. Cure mental with physical distance first

Escape (physically) and cure with fresh air. If it's attacking your mental health, feel free to refresh for better performance. Even though it can pause your career, curing mental is always better, because working without any therapy mostly leads to poor performance.

It can be done with 1-2 hours exercise like running/cycling — but if mental issue is critical, feel free to travel during a couple of days.


## 02. Accept the Uncontrollable

After recovery, draw a clear line: what is within your control, or not. Then, even though it's painful, consciously stop spending effort beyond that line.

You can control your response, your craft, and your next small move. But you cannot control the situation, others' decisions, or timing. Those are not your fault.


## 03. Escape from Sunk Cost

If you finished drawing a clear line, we can rewind our decision making. Our life consists of constant/continuous decision making. Each one can be good or worse, and sometimes, we might realize after facing consequential outcome (bias).

For sure, when outcome is good then everything is fine — but if it's not, you might get caught in thoughts like "I should have moved to other choice..". It can be really frustrating if it's unable to revert.

Instead of regret, try to find uncovered paths you might have missed at the moment of decision. If your decision was made carefully, each candidate choice likely opens its own mid-term trajectory — treat those as trajectories worth revisiting with fresh eyes.

_Plus, please trust your skills/mindset with humble stance._
